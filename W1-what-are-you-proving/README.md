# What Are You Proving?

Assignment: *What Are You Proving?* (General AI Fluency, Week 1)
Author: Miguel Garcia Roman

## Proof statement

> **I take an undocumented backend system and ship the small, reliable tool that
> makes it usable — reading the wire, not waiting for docs.**

- **One claim:** I can reverse-engineer an opaque backend (an API, a server-action
  app, an auth flow) and build a thin, working client for it. Not "full-stack,"
  not "AI enthusiast" — this one thing.
- **One person:** a founding or early backend engineer at a small AI product
  company, the one who owns three half-documented integrations and needs someone
  who can take one off their plate and make it *just work* without hand-holding.
- **One action:** read one repo of mine, then email me.

## Why this needs to exist

A CV says "Python, APIs, Docker." So does everyone's. What it can't show is the
part that actually matters for the person above: that when I hit a system with no
docs, I don't stall — I read the traffic, find the seams, and ship something that
runs. A public repo of small tools that each solve a real, messy integration
proves that in a way a bullet list never will.

## How I got here (AI as thinking partner, not author)

I had the model interview me instead of write for me. The claims it kept trying
to hand me were the generic ones:

- *"Backend engineer who builds scalable, production-ready services."* — Rejected.
  True of ten thousand people; proves nothing; "scalable" is a word I'd have to
  defend and can't yet.
- *"Full-stack developer comfortable across the stack."* — Rejected. "Comfortable
  across" is hiding three skills behind *and*. It describes a portfolio, not mine.
- *"I build tools that integrate with real systems."* — Closer, still soft.
  "Integrate with real systems" could be reading a documented REST API.

The pushback that made it mine: *what did you do this week that most "backend
engineers" would not have done?* Answer — I pulled a session cookie out of a
browser keyring, decoded a Next.js Server Action off the wire, and built a CLI
against an app that publishes no API at all. That is the narrow, true claim. The
statement above could only describe *my* proof, not any portfolio.

## The test it has to pass

If a page can be summarized as "smart person, various skills," it fails. Mine has
to read as: *this specific person will read an undocumented system and hand me a
tool that works.* One claim, one reader, one thing to click.
